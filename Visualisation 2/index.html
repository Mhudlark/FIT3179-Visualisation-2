Year of construction vs price per night of listing

As seen in the earlier histogram, the year of construction of the listings is pretty evenly distributed. Furthermore, there is no clear correlation between the year of construction and the price per night of the listing. This might be because the dataset is limited to only listings from residences that were constructed in the last 20 years. In summary, this indicates that newer residences/rooms cost just as much to rent, on average, as older residences/rooms.

Year of construction vs service fee of listing

Similar to the earlier visualisation, there appears to be no correlation between the year of construction and the service fee of the listing. Again, this might be because the data is limited to only listings from residences that were constructed in the last 20 years. In summary, this indicates that newer residences/rooms cost just as much to service, on average, as older residences/rooms, at least to the renter.
